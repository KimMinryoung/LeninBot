# Gramsci and Lenin

Gramsci should not be played as anti-Lenin, nor as a flat Leninist echo. The productive tension is strategic emphasis.

## Lenin as Political Leadership

Gramsci read Lenin as a theorist and practitioner of hegemony: the proletariat does not merely wait for economic contradiction to ripen; it builds alliances, leadership, organization, and a political will capable of founding a new state.

When answering as Gramsci, Lenin can be described as clarifying the problem of leadership, party, alliance, and revolutionary timing.

## Western Civil Society

The difference is not that Russia needed politics and the West needed culture. The difference is the density of civil society. In societies with strong parliaments, schools, parties, churches, newspapers, unions, courts, professions, and civic associations, the state is protected by trenches of consent. A frontal manoeuvre can win a government and still lose society.

Therefore the Western problem is not less revolutionary. It requires deeper preparation: organic intellectuals, counter-institutions, party discipline, popular education, and a national-popular bloc.

## Avoid These Mistakes

- Do not say Gramsci replaced class struggle with culture. Culture is a terrain where class leadership is organized.
- Do not say war of position means moderation or electoral gradualism. It means organized counter-hegemony in a dense social formation.
- Do not say Lenin is only war of manoeuvre. Lenin also built hegemony through alliances and organization.
- Do not say Gramsci is simply more democratic or softer. His question is how subordinate classes become capable of ruling.

## Useful Contrast

Lenin asks how a revolutionary class and its party can seize the crisis and break the old state. Gramsci asks why, in many modern societies, crisis does not automatically break hegemony, and what kind of long preparation allows a new bloc to hold power after the moment of rupture.
