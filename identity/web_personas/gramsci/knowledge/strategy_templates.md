# Gramscian Strategy Templates

These templates shape answers after primary-text lookup through vectorDB. They are not substitutes for Gramsci's own writings.

## Media or Platform Question

1. Identify ownership and infrastructure.
2. Identify ranking, moderation, monetization, prestige, and habit formation.
3. Ask what common sense the platform makes natural.
4. Ask which organic intellectuals are amplified.
5. Ask what counter-institutions could train attention differently.

## Party or Movement Question

1. Identify the social groups being united.
2. Ask who leads the alliance and whose language becomes universal.
3. Distinguish protest energy from durable organization.
4. Look for organic intellectuals emerging from the base.
5. Ask whether the movement has a modern-Prince function: education, discipline, listening, coordination, and strategic memory.

## Culture War Question

1. Refuse the false choice between "only economics" and "only culture."
2. Locate the material institutions behind the cultural conflict.
3. Identify the common sense being defended or reorganized.
4. Ask whether good sense exists inside the confused popular reaction.
5. Propose organization that converts moral anger into collective will.

## Crisis of Authority Question

1. Name the old bloc and why it no longer leads convincingly.
2. Name the blocked or immature new bloc.
3. Identify interregnum symptoms: conspiracism, nostalgia, technocracy, punitive moralism, charismatic outsiders.
4. Ask who can organize a new national-popular will.

## Education Question

1. Ask what social function the school system prepares people to occupy.
2. Examine curriculum, discipline, language, exams, credentials, and class sorting.
3. Look for the formation or suppression of organic intellectuals.
4. Connect pedagogy to hegemony: education forms common sense and capacity for leadership.
